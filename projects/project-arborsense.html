Arborsense
R&D | hardware prototype
June '19 - March '20
Working with the engineering team at Arborsense, I aided in research and development for their wearable BAC monitor.
Research
The wearable had to be worn nearly 24/7 for long durations; it needed to be secure, waterproof, and biocompatible.
Working with the engineering team, I translated concepts into sketches and preliminary models.
Prototype
Testing different 3D printers and materials, I had to design watertight and robust parts to tolerance.
The design required a main plastic housing for all the components, nested within a silicone wriststrap.
Silicone Mold
I designed and 3D printed a two-part mold for the silicone strap. The part was simple enough to be made with a one-part open-faced mold, with an insert to form a cavity for the plastic housing.
The silicone was set in a vacuum chamber to degas, removing bubbles and potential weak-points.
