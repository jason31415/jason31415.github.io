Remediate Phone Box
user research | hardware prototype
Jan 19 - Present
Team: Chase Zheng (Marketing), Steve Song (Project Management), Xuan Jin (UI/UX), Jason Pi (ID).

For a product development course aimed at building healthy living habits in preadolescents, our group focused on mediating phone usage before bedtime.

User research

Over the course of a semester, we worked with parents and children to help define our pain points and refine our prototype.

Photo by Roland Graf

With the help of the parents and children, we were able to pick a few key features: an alarm clock, a locking mechanism, and an emergency unlatch button.

Figuring out the overall form and latching mechanism. The lid had to be flush when closed, and electronically latched.

Prototype 2

Using an arduino, I was able to control the latching of the lid with a modified servo motor. I fitted a button underneath a spring that would be compressed by the closed lid, allowing the detection of the lid closing, and for the lid to pop up when the servo is released.

Prototype 3

After our second design review, we decided to add in a LCD clockface, UV sanitization, and a night light. Along with the inclusion of a few prefabricated parts, this ended up changing our overall design language.

Driven by an arduino hidden under the phone tray, I was able to control the latching mode and night light with a single glowing arcade button. The goal was to make the device look more playful, with a customizeable tray that could be lined with different patterned fabrics.

Moving Forward

After seeing legitimate demand from our target market, our group decided to keep working on our product and try to bring it to market. As of now, we've incorporated under Remediate LLC and received over $18,000 in funding.
