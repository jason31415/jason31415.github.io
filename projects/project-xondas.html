Xondas Lamp
R&D | prototype
Mar 19 - June 20
Designing a striking lamp that would also serve to showcase the Xondas' wireless power technology.
Concept Generation
The prompt was to design a floating lamp that would exaggerate the lack of wires, and appeal to tech enthusiasts.
The emphasis on the floating bulb showed off the extent of their wireless tech. Having a larger floating body would exaggerate this, but a balance had to be struck between how heavy the top could be and how high it could float.
Monolith from Stanley Kubrick's 2001: A Space Odyssey
The device had to be simple, yet stunning, and seamlessly incorporate the aspect of levitation into the design. I wanted it to be monolithic, with distinct planes and a bold silhouette. It had quietly to inspire a sense of awe, like the Monolith in 2001: A Space Odyssey
In early designs, I looked at different ways of incorporating an interface into the product. Switches, knobs, and dials were used, but ultimately detracted from the overall form.
Landing on a Design
After experimenting with a levitation kit, I found that suspension would be possible at an angle, which further accentuated the gravity defying look.
We decided to levitate the bulb at an angle to further accentuate the floating effect. The surface directly beneath would be concaved and matte, diffusing the light directed below.
Prototyping
Working 3D printed parts, I was able to test the levitation and define the dimensions of custom PCBs.
Floating Bulb
Designing the floating body was one of the toughest challenges. The bulb had to hold all the circuitry and hardware while still staying afloat. I had to constantly callibrate different sized bulbs while tweaking the magnetic field.
Finalizing the form
After finding the limits to what the magnet could suspend, I was able to finalize the size of the bulb. Working with the engineering team, we were able to shrink the footprint of the circuitboard to fit a smaller base, thus maintaining an appealing size ratio between the base and the bulb.
The final design incorporated a touch capacitive ring and a physical knob on the floating bulb.
Final Prototypes
Working with 3D printed parts, I added a resin coating, primer, and another layer of paint to create a matte white finish.
